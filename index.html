JERUSALEM, ISRAEL

Yad Vashem

Tourists flock to the Old City of Jerusalem to witness and experience the most sacred Christian sites in the world; Among a number of sites, there's the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, the Wailing Wall, and the Holocaust Remembrance Museum (Yad Vashem). Pilgrims visit the Church of the Holy Sepulchre that was built over the sight of the crucifixion and houses the Tomb of Jesus. No pilgrimage to Jerusalem is complete without a visit to the Wailing Wall. Jews often wail as they offer prayers to the wall, which is a stark reminder to the Jewish people of the destruction of two of their temples during Roman rule. A must-see for tourists is the Yad Vashem, where one can view monuments, artifacts, memorial sites, and personal accounts of holocaust survivors.

SOCHI, RUSSIA

Krasnaya Polyana

Sochi is a sight to behold. Although it is not as famous as Russia's capital city of Moscow, it boasts of many mountain sceneries, beaches, rivers, and alps during wintertime. Krasnaya Polyana, in particular, homes plenty of tourist spots in the city. It has enough hotels that can accomodate thousands of tourists and serves as a dream vacation destination to many. Sochi has also hosted sporting events like the F1 grand prix and the winter olympics.

NEW YORK, U.S.A.

9/11 Memorial and Museum

New York is famous for its skyscrapers but visitors will find themselves looking down to marvel at the 9/11 Memorial and Museum. Built on what used to be the World Trade Center complex, this mostly underground installation has become a staple in New York must-visit lists especially for first timers. The memorial to the deadliest terror attack on the US was opened to the public on September 11, 2011, a decade after the tragic event.
