JERUSALEM, ISRAEL

Church of the Holy Sepulchre and the Wailing Wall

Pilgrims and tourists flock to the Old City of Jerusalem to witness and experience two of the most sacred Christian sites in the world; The Church of the Holy Sepulchre and the Wailing Wall. The church was built over the sight of the crucifixion and houses the Tomb of Jesus. Christian or not, it is an awe-inspiring experience to see this place up close. No pilgrimage to Jerusalem is complete without a visit to the Wailing Wall. Made of large stones that weigh up to 8 tons, the wall is a stark reminder to the Jewish people of the destruction of two of their temples during Roman rule. Hence, Jews often wail as they offer prayers to the wall.

NEW YORK, U.S.A.

9/11 Memorial and Museum

New York is famous for its skyscrapers but visitors will find themselves looking down to marvel at the 9/11 Memorial and Museum. Built on what used to be the World Trade Center complex, this mostly underground installation has become a staple in New York must-visit lists especially for first timers. The memorial to the deadliest terror attack on the US was opened to the public on September 11, 2011, a decade after the tragic event. ‘Tribute in Light,’ a magnificent light display gets illuminated on 9/11 every year to commemorate those who lost their lives in the attack.
